East Africa Ecosystems

Explore Uganda

From the glaciated peaks of the Rwenzori Mountains to the thick equatorial jungles of Bwindi, Uganda offers some of Africa's most diverse landscapes.

⛰

Rwenzori Range

Known as the 'Mountains of the Moon', the Rwenzori range boasts glaciated slopes and high-alpine bogs. Traversing the Kilembe Trail provides stunning views of Margherita Peak, standing at 5,109m.

🦍

Bwindi Impenetrable

A ancient rainforest home to half of the world's remaining Mountain Gorillas. Its dense canopy, steep valleys, and muddy trails offer a high-immersion wildlife sanctuary.

🐆

Queen Elizabeth Park

Featuring sweeping grass savannas, volcanic craters, and channels. Home to the tree-climbing lions of Ishasha, giant elephants, hippos, and over 600 bird species.

🌋

Mount Elgon Base

Home to the world's largest volcanic base. A UNESCO Man & Biosphere Reserve filled with pristine waterfalls, volcanic calderas, coffee farms, and rich community culture.

Accessible Tourism

Inclusive Safaris for All

We believe the beauty of East Africa should be open to everyone. We adapt our operations, vehicles, and lodges to support travelers with physical, sensory, or age-related limitations.

♿

Mobility & Wheelchair Safaris

We solve local terrain challenges with modified 4x4 safari cruisers featuring ramp boarding systems, heavy-duty floor tie-downs, and wide-door entries. We selectively book lodges with paved, step-free access and roll-in showers.

  • Custom 4x4 lift/ramp vehicles
  • Roll-in showers & accessible lodge reviews
  • Step-free ground itineraries
🪑

Primate Trekking Sedan Chairs

Endangered mountain gorillas reside in steep, muddy rainforest valleys in Bwindi. We coordinate specialized steel-reinforced sedan chairs and traditional stretchers, carried securely by a team of 6 to 8 experienced local porters.

  • Secured porter team (6 to 8 porters)
  • Pre-scheduled Bwindi park coordinate mapping
  • Comprehensive safety harnesses ($300-$500 pricing)
👁

Sensory & Visual Impairment Guiding

We curate tactile and auditory-rich itineraries focusing on local sounds, forest bird calls, wood workshops, musical performances, and physical touch points, supported by description-focused guiding.

  • Sensory forest walks & bird-song logs
  • Braille-equivalent and high-contrast briefings
  • Tactile cultural encounters
🦻

Deaf & Hearing Impairment Guides

We feature professional guides trained in international sign language and standard wildlife visual codes. We provide modified visual briefing booklets and safety cards to ensure absolute clarity during game drives.

  • Sign-language certified safari guides
  • Visual safety aids & animal checksheets
  • Interactive visual route boards
👴

Seniors & Relaxed Pace Safaris

Ideal for multi-generational families and senior travelers. We build slower itineraries containing extra rest periods, ground-floor accessible rooms, and scheduled regional flights to avoid long, tiring road drives.

  • Slow-paced private itineraries
  • Domestic flight links (Entebbe-Kihihi-Kasese)
  • First-aid and emergency oxygen systems on board

Corporate Integrity

Our Founding History

Founded in 2008 in Kampala, Uganda, Maleng Travel is a registered private Destination Management Company (DMC) and tour operator. Over the past 18 years, we have built a reputation for local logistical excellence and ecological commitment.

While our physical headquarters shares space with sister company Master Wood Investments Ltd at Plot 1 Ofungi Rise, Kitintale, our financial, booking, licensing, and client communication services are completely isolated to protect travelers and ensure professional compliance.
